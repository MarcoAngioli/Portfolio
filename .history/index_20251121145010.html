About me

I am Marco Angioli, a PhD candidate in Technologies of Information and Communication (ICT) – Electronic Engineering curriculum – at Sapienza University of Rome. My research lies at the intersection of digital hardware design for artificial intelligence, hyperdimensional computing / vector symbolic architectures, and embedded AI for resource-constrained edge and IoT devices.

I work on hardware–software co-design for efficient learning systems: from contextual bandit algorithms running on embedded RISC-V processors to variable-latency arithmetic units and dedicated accelerators for Hyperdimensional Computing, with a focus on performance, energy efficiency, and reliability in realistic workloads.

Science

Efficient LinearUCB for Embedded Learning Systems

ACM Transactions on Embedded Computing Systems, 2025.
Algorithmic and hardware techniques (Sherman–Morrison–Woodbury and vector acceleration) to implement LinearUCB contextual bandits efficiently on embedded devices.
Read the paper

Efficient Hyperdimensional Computing with Modular Composite Representations

Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence.
Revisits MCR as a high-dimensional model with improved information capacity and hardware efficiency, including the first dedicated accelerator and extensive evaluation.

HD-CB & HD-CB_BIN for Contextual Bandits

Preprint + IJCNN 2025.
Introduces Hyperdimensional Contextual Bandits and a lightweight binary variant (HD-CB_BIN), showing competitive or superior performance vs LinUCB with large speedups and reduced complexity on embedded platforms.

AeneasHDC: Automatic Framework for HDC on FPGAs

IEEE WCCI 2024.
Open-source framework to deploy HDC models in software and hardware, enabling design-space exploration across accuracy, memory, execution time, power, and area.

Variable Latency Division Schemes

IEEE Transactions on Computers + PRIME 2024.
New data-dependent variable latency integer division algorithm and its integration in a RISC-V vector accelerator, improving performance and energy with modest hardware overhead.

Fault-Tolerant Hardware Acceleration for Edge Computing

Electronics, 2023.
Combines fault-tolerant RISC-V cores with configurable vector accelerators, studying the trade-offs between performance, hardware utilization, and vulnerability under faults.

Projects

  • Design of an 8-bit FMA (Fused Multiply-Add) Unit in VHDL – MSc project.
    Design and optimization of an 8-bit FMA unit using VHDL, synthesis on Xilinx devices with Vivado, and evaluation of performance and hardware resources.
  • Motion Detection on Zybo Z7-20 with Hardware Acceleration – MSc project.
    Implementation of a high-performance motion detection pipeline on the Zybo Z7-20 board using an external Pcam 5C camera and hardware-accelerated kernels.
  • Predictive Maintenance with Recurrent Neural Networks – MSc project.
    RNNs (LSTM, Bi-LSTM, GRU) in Python to predict failures in aircraft turbines from sensor time series, as part of a Machine Learning for Signal Processing course.
  • Predictive Maintenance on Train Motors – MSc project.
    SVM with RBF kernel and genetic optimization to detect potential failures in train compressors based on pressure samples.
  • Rotation Degree Estimation with CNNs – MSc project.
    Convolutional neural network to regress the rotation angle of handwritten digits in the MNIST dataset, exploring robustness to geometric transformations.
